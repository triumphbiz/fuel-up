Sunday Batch Prep

🏆 The #1 habit that makes everything easier: Spend 60–90 minutes on Sunday getting ahead. You don't need to cook everything — just prep the basics so weekday decisions are already made for you. You start work at 6am — this is the only way to make sure you eat properly all week.

🍳 Step 1 — Boil Eggs

  • Put 6–8 eggs in a pot, cover with cold water
  • Bring to boil, then turn to medium for 9 minutes
  • Run under cold water, peel, store in a container in the fridge
  • Lasts 5 days — grab one any time you're hungry

🍗 Step 2 — Cook Chicken

  • Put 500g–1kg chicken breast in a baking tray
  • Spray with olive oil, add mixed herbs and a pinch of salt
  • Bake at 200°C for 22–25 minutes
  • Slice and store — use in salads, wraps, or with rice all week
  • Lasts 4 days in the fridge

🥗 Step 3 — Build the Salad Base

  • Shred ¼ red cabbage, grate 2 carrots
  • Slice capsicum, cucumber, celery, shallots
  • Wash and dry spinach and lettuce
  • Halve cherry tomatoes
  • Mix everything in a big container — grab a portion each day
  • Keeps 4 days. Add dressing only when eating

🫙 Step 4 — Make Overnight Oats

  • Get 5 small jars or containers
  • Add 60g oats, 200ml low-fat milk, 1 scoop protein powder to each
  • Stir, put lid on, fridge overnight
  • In the morning at 5:30am — grab a jar and go. Zero prep.
  • Add berries or a banana in the morning if you want

🍲 Step 5 — One Big Batch Meal

  • Make a TM31 meal or a big pot of chilli, curry, or bolognese
  • Divide into 4–5 containers for lunches or dinners
  • Label with the day so you eat the oldest first
  • Freeze 2 if you won't eat them all by Wednesday

🥤 Step 6 — Portion Snacks

  • Pre-bag almonds into small zip-lock bags (20g each)
  • Put Greek yoghurt cups in the front of the fridge
  • Line up your protein bars and Babybels so they're easy to grab
  • Pack your lunch bag the night before — not the morning
  • If it's easy to grab, you'll grab it instead of junk
Set a Sunday alarm for 3pm — call it "Kitchen Hour". Even just doing steps 1, 2 and 4 is enough to make your whole week easier. You start work at 6am — getting ahead on Sunday is how you win.

Protein Goal

Aim for 150–180g of protein per day. This sounds like a lot but it keeps you full, stops you losing muscle while you lose fat, and it's easier than you think when you eat the right foods.

🙋 Quick Tips That Actually Work

  • 💧 Drink a glass of water before every meal — it fills you up and you'll eat less naturally.
  • 🚫 Don't skip lunch. If you miss lunch, you'll be ravenous at night and eat everything in sight.
  • 🍽️ Use a medium-sized plate — a full medium plate looks satisfying. A half-full big plate looks sad.
  • 🥩 Protein first — eat your chicken/meat/eggs before the bread or rice. You'll eat less overall.
  • 📱 If you want to track properly, try MyFitnessPal (free). Scan the barcode and it logs automatically.
  • 🏆 Progress not perfection. One bad day doesn't undo a week of good eating. Just get back on track at the next meal.

TM31 Beginner's Guide

The TM31 looks complicated but it's basically a bowl that chops, cooks, and stirs for you. This guide tells you what every setting means in plain English so you can follow any recipe confidently.

⚡ Speed Guide

Speed controls how fast the blades spin. Here's what each level does:

SpeedWhat it does
Speed 1Very gentle stir — sauces, soups
Speed 2–3Slow mixing — risotto, porridge
Speed 4–5Chopping soft things — onion, herbs
Speed 7–8Chopping firm veg — carrots, nuts
Speed 10Pureeing soups, blending
TurboCrushing ice, very fast blitz
ReverseStirs WITHOUT cutting — for whole ingredients

🌡️ Temperature Guide

The TM31 heats food as it mixes. Temp goes up to 100°C (Varoma is steam level):

TempWhat it does
37°CBody temp — warming milk, yoghurt
60°CGentle heat — melting butter, warming
80°CSimmering — sauces
100°CBoiling — cooking rice, pasta
VaromaSteam — cooking meat/veg above the bowl

🧺 The Varoma Tray

The Varoma is the big tray that sits on top of the TM31 lid. When the bowl is heating at Varoma temperature, steam rises and cooks whatever is in the tray.

Use it for: steaming chicken, fish, broccoli, carrots, eggs. Everything stays moist and tender — and healthy because nothing is fried.

Tip: Always put a lid on the Varoma tray while cooking.

🥣 The Simmering Basket

A plastic basket that sits inside the bowl. Use it to cook rice or steam vegetables inside the liquid — it acts like a strainer so you can lift everything out cleanly when done.

Rinse your rice first, put it in the basket, add water to the bowl, and cook. When it's done, use the spatula to lift the basket out — the TM31 spatula has a hook at the end specifically designed to clip into the basket handle so you can lift it safely without burning yourself.

🔑 Key Beginner Tips

Never go above Speed 4 while hot — hot liquid splashes and burns. Speed 1–2 for hot food.

Always replace the MC cap when blending hot things — hold a cloth over it too.

Use Reverse when you want to stir without cutting chunks.

Don't overfill the bowl — max line is 2L.

🧼 Cleaning

Dead easy:

  • 1. Add 1L warm water to the bowl
  • 2. Add a drop of dish soap
  • 3. Run 10 seconds at Speed 8
  • 4. Rinse and done

Stubborn food? Let soapy water soak 5 minutes first.

📏 Measuring in the TM31

The bowl has a built-in scale — zero it (tare button), add ingredients directly. No measuring cups needed.

Quick conversions:
1 cup of water = 250g
1 tbsp = roughly 15g
Stock cube + 500g water = 500ml stock
